The Monty Hall problem is a brain teaser, in the form of a probability puzzle, loosely based on the American television game show Let's Make a Deal and named after its original host, Monty Hall.

Let's play a game to understand it.

Below is a set of three doors. Behind one of these doors is a prize, the other two doors do not! The prize is randomly set behind these doors, and hence the probability of any door having the prize is 1/3 and not having it is 2/3.
